Image Based Plant Disease Detection A Comparison Of Deep Learning And Classical Machine Learning

Overview of Project

Plant disease detection has been a critical area of research due to its significant impact on agricultural productivity and food security. Traditionally, visual inspection by experts was the primary method for identifying plant diseases. However, this approach is time-consuming, subjective, and not scalable for large-scale farming. With advancements in technology, computational approaches have emerged, offering more efficient and accurate solutions. Modern methods leverage image processing and machine learning techniques to automate the detection process, significantly enhancing the speed and reliability of disease identification.

Statement of the Problem

The efficient management of plant health is a critical issue in modern agriculture, as plant diseases can significantly diminish crop yields and quality. Traditional methods for disease identification, which often rely on manual inspection by agricultural experts, are not only labor-intensive but also susceptible to inconsistencies and errors. As a result, there is a pressing need for automated solutions that can accurately and swiftly diagnose plant diseases to mitigate these challenges.

Dataset Description

The PlantVillage dataset, available on Kaggle, comprises color images of various plant species affected by different diseases. This dataset is widely used for research in plant disease detection due to its comprehensive collection of images, which include both healthy and diseased plant leaves. The dataset contains images for different types of plants such as apple, grape, peach, and more, each with corresponding disease categories.
